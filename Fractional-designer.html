The model

What "fractional" actually means

Fractional is not freelance. I don't wait for briefs, I don't deliver isolated assets, and I don't clock hours. I work the way a senior employee works, taking on the mental load of scoping, prioritising, and carrying design from discovery all the way through to implementation, just not at full-time capacity.

Think of it as ~60% of a full-time senior designer: present on Slack, in planning sessions, in engineering syncs, in user research. Integrated, not adjacent.

FAQ

Isn't this just freelancing with a different name?

No. A freelancer waits for a brief, delivers a defined output, and moves on. I join your team. I'm in your planning sessions, I help define what needs to be designed and why, I work with your engineers through implementation, and I take responsibility for the design function, not just individual deliverables. The mental ownership is entirely different.

What if we need you and you're tied up with another client?

This rarely happens in practice because the model is async-friendly and output-oriented rather than time-bound. I'm available on Slack for quick decisions and present in the meetings that matter. I keep my client roster intentionally small and only take on engagements where I know I can deliver meaningfully.

Will you actually develop deep context about our product?

Yes, and this is where the fractional model differs from consulting. I'm not parachuting in for a sprint. I embed over months, building up institutional knowledge about your users, your technical architecture, your design decisions and why they were made. Over time I become one of the people who knows your product best.

What if this doesn't work out?

We agree on terms that feel right for you from the start, whether that's a short trial or jumping straight into a longer commitment. The structure is designed to give you confidence, not lock you in. I'm selective about who I work with precisely because I'm looking for this to work long-term.

What if we want more security that you won't just leave?

Commitment terms are fully flexible, including matching the notice period you'd have with a full-time employee, whenever that feels right. More importantly, I'm here because I want a long-term engagement. I'm selective about the companies I work with precisely because I'm not looking for short rotations. I'm looking for products I believe in and want to grow with.

How is this better than hiring a full-time designer?

A full-time senior designer who can self-direct, own the design function, and work well with engineers is genuinely hard to find and expensive to hire. This model gives you exactly that, structured in a way that works for where you are right now, with flexibility to evolve as you grow.

How does billing work?

A fixed monthly retainer, paid in advance. No hourly tracking, no project-by-project negotiation. You get consistent access to me and I plan my time around your needs. The fee reflects a roughly 60% capacity engagement relative to a full-time senior designer salary, making the value straightforward to benchmark.

Have you done this before?

Yes. I run Creoscope, my independent design practice, specifically structured around this model. I've worked this way with SaaS companies across e-signing, document management, and operations software, taking products from early concept through to production. It's not an experiment; it's how I work.

What happens if you eventually want to hire a full-time designer?

That's a great outcome and I'd help make it happen. I've helped startups hire and onboard designers before, so I know what good looks like at different stages. By the time you're ready, I'll know your product, your design system, and exactly what the role needs to look like. I can help you define the brief, review candidates, and ensure a smooth handover. The fractional model is often the best way to get to that point with strong foundations already in place.

What does "integrated" actually look like day-to-day?

I join relevant planning and sprint ceremonies, I'm reachable on Slack for async questions and quick feedback, I attend product reviews and key stakeholder sessions, and I work directly with engineers during implementation. You won't need to brief me. I'll stay across what's happening and flag design decisions proactively.
